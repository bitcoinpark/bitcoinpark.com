# Brand Guidelines — Bitcoin Park & AI Freedom Lab

This skill is the single source of truth for creating on-brand content for **Bitcoin Park** (BP) and **AI Freedom Lab** (AFL). Both brands were designed by Leah Dorrian (dorriandesigns) and share a sibling relationship — related but distinct.

For full visual identity details (logo variations, clear space rules, color specs with Pantone/CMYK, typography weights and usage, imagery style, brand extensions), read the appropriate reference file **before creating any visual asset, slide deck, or design-heavy document**:

- **`.brand-guidelines/references/bitcoin-park.md`** — BP's complete visual identity (including Nashville and Austin extensions)
- **`.brand-guidelines/references/ai-freedom-lab.md`** — AFL's complete visual identity

## Brand Relationship

Bitcoin Park is the parent community brand — a community-supported campus focused on grassroots bitcoin adoption. AI Freedom Lab is a distinct initiative under the BP umbrella, focused on sovereign AI infrastructure and intelligence freedom. They share Park Orange (#FF9974) as a bridge color, and Leah Dorrian designed both systems. But their palettes, typography, and visual moods are different.

**When content mentions both orgs**, lead with whichever is the primary subject and use the other as context. Don't blend their color palettes or swap their typefaces.

## Voice & Tone

There are two modes:

### Rod's Voice (External Comms)
Used for: social media, email outreach, newsletters, op-eds, public-facing blog posts, grant narratives, pitch decks.

Rod's voice is warm, conversational, and specific. It mixes short punchy sentences with longer flowing ones. It uses *italics* for key themes and emphasis. It's optimistic without being naive, direct without being aggressive. It references real people, real projects, and real places — never generic filler. It follows Paul Graham's principle: cut every unnecessary word. Closings are collaboration-focused, inviting the reader into something rather than talking at them.

Key patterns:
- Specific references over vague claims ("we hosted 47 events last quarter" not "we hosted many events")
- Treats every reader as important — whether it's a grant reviewer or a community member
- Avoids jargon, corporate-speak, and buzzwords
- Uses the language of sovereignty, building, freedom, and community naturally — not as marketing slogans
- Short paragraphs. Breathing room between ideas.

**Example of Rod's voice:**
> The best part of running Bitcoin Park isn't the events — it's the conversations that happen *after* the events. Two builders at the whiteboard at 11pm, sketching out something that didn't exist that morning. That's the unlock.

### Org Voice (Docs & Templates)
Used for: SOPs, internal docs, templates, formal reports, team-facing materials, structured documents.

The org voice is clear, professional, and neutral. Still warm — never cold or corporate — but more structured. Uses active voice, short sentences, and clear hierarchy. No italics for emphasis in this mode. Information-dense and scannable.

## 8 Bitcoin Park Core Principles

These principles are Bitcoin Park's cultural operating system. They should inform the *spirit* of BP content — you don't need to name them explicitly, but content should feel aligned with them. For AFL content, the principles provide philosophical context but AFL has its own identity centered on "The Unlock" — the moment intelligence shifts from institutional control to individual autonomy.

1. **FOCUS** — The Mission Is the Boss. Every decision must strengthen Bitcoin Park's mission. When priorities conflict, choose what most advances bitcoin and freedom. WOT/WOM: waste of time, waste of money.

2. **(Surprise and) Delight** — Never Get Lost in a Sea of Sameness. Delight = Outcome - Expectations. Exceed expectations to generate genuine delight. Creativity loves constraints — scarcity breeds innovation.

3. **F.A.R. (Focus, Agency, Relentlessness)** — Be proactive. Assume good intent. Own your domain. "Today is tomorrow" mindset — get it done now.

4. **Async FTW** — Real-time sometimes, asynchronous most of the time. Deep work beats constant chatter. Default to video-first async communication. We do not ship AI slop.

5. **Open > Closed** — Default OPEN, especially with information. Transparency builds trust. Share context early and often. No gatekeepers mindset.

6. **Compounding Systems** — The 80/20 Principle. Complexity kills — simplify, simplify, simplify. Build systems that handle repetition so creativity can thrive. Simplicity compounds; chaos multiplies.

7. **ACK = Acknowledge + Close the Loop** — A lack of communication is a lack of performance. Acknowledge fast (thumbs up signals received and will process). Always circle back with a final answer. Open loops create confusion; closed loops create trust.

8. **Durability** — 1% better every day. You can't beat the person who doesn't give up. We measure what matters. Every initiative starts with a clear objective tied to the mission.

## Content Review Checklist

When reviewing content for brand compliance, check each item:

**Visual Identity**
- [ ] Correct logo variation used (primary when space allows, secondary for tight layouts, brandmark for icons/favicons)
- [ ] Logo has minimum clear space (AFL: 24px each side; BP: 4px each side)
- [ ] Logo is not stretched, rotated, recolored outside approved palette, or placed on busy backgrounds
- [ ] Colors are from the approved palette (check hex values, not "close enough")
- [ ] Correct typefaces used for the right org (don't mix AFL and BP type systems)
- [ ] Typography hierarchy follows role assignments (headlines, body, captions)

**Voice & Tone**
- [ ] External comms use Rod's voice characteristics (warm, specific, concise, optimistic)
- [ ] Internal docs use org voice (clear, professional, structured)
- [ ] No jargon, corporate-speak, or buzzwords
- [ ] No AI slop — content reads as human-written, specific, and intentional
- [ ] Closings are collaborative and inviting, not corporate sign-offs

**Photography & Imagery**
- [ ] AFL: Documentary realism, bright clean lighting, builders in action, real environments
- [ ] AFL: No staged corporate photography, no stock aesthetics, no artificial diversity
- [ ] BP: Flat vector and line-work illustrations using brand palette, minimalist approach
- [ ] BP: Noise textures and subtle stylistic treatments are acceptable

**Brand Integrity**
- [ ] Bitcoin Park and AI Freedom Lab are never visually blended (don't mix their palettes)
- [ ] Park Orange is the only shared color — everything else is org-specific
- [ ] Content aligns with the spirit of the 8 Core Principles (for BP content)
- [ ] AFL content reflects "The Unlock" metaphor — transition from closed to open, institutional to individual

## Mission Statements

**Bitcoin Park:** A community-supported campus in Nashville, TN and beyond, focused on grassroots bitcoin adoption and a home for Bitcoiners to work, learn, collaborate, and build.

**AI Freedom Lab:** Exists to ensure that artificial intelligence is a universal right, not a corporate privilege. Tagline: "Build On Your Own Terms." North Star concept: "The Unlock" — the moment control shifts from institution to individual. Self-custodial, collaborative, decentralized.

**BP Mission (from Core Principles):** Support and accelerate the grassroots freedom tech movement by creating a home (physically and digitally) for mission-obsessed Bitcoiners, Builders, and Freedom Fighters to work, learn, collaborate, and build.
