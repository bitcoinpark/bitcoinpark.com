# AI Freedom Lab — Complete Visual Identity Reference

Source: AI Freedom Lab Brandbook (designed by Leah Dorrian / dorriandesigns, February 2025)

---

## Brand Philosophy

AI Freedom Lab visualizes intelligence freedom through the act of liberation: minds breaking free from centralized control, gaining custody over their own cognition.

The brand identity is rooted in the transition from closed to open:
- **Self-Custodial:** You hold the keys to your intelligence.
- **Collaborative:** A radiating system where nodes connect to empower the collective.
- **Decentralized:** Breaking the silos of corporate control to distribute power to the edges.

Core metaphor: "The Unlock" — the precise moment where control shifts from the institution to the individual, and where 8 billion minds are granted the agency to create, reason, and evolve without permission.

Key quote from the brand: "Not your memory, not your brain." Just as Bitcoin liberated the ledger, AFL is liberating the processor.

## Logo System

### Primary Logo (Horizontal)
- Custom graphic symbol (butterfly/wings brandmark) + stylized letterwork in P22 Mackinac Pro
- The wings form a subtle 'F' shape — a nod to "Freedom"
- Use this variation most frequently when space allows
- Minimum clear space: 24px on all four sides
- Can be used in any primary or secondary brand color

### Secondary Logo (Stacked)
- Same elements reconfigured vertically — brandmark above wordmark
- Use when the horizontal layout is restricted by space or composition
- Same 24px clear space rule applies

### Brandmark (Icon Only)
- The butterfly/wings symbol alone
- Reserved for: favicons, app icons, social media profiles, graphic stamps in product UI

### Wordmark (Logotype Only)
- "AI Freedom Lab" text only in P22 Mackinac Pro
- Ideal for: editorial layouts, website navigation, formal documents

### Logo Don'ts
- Do not stretch or distort proportions
- Do not use random/unapproved colors
- Do not rotate
- Do not alter the letterwork
- Do not change or modify the brandmark
- Do not place on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast (use solid color block or darkened overlay)

## Typography

### Primary Typeface: Hanken Grotesk
A contemporary sans-serif that serves as the cornerstone of the visual identity. Functional and clear, echoing the geometry of industrial design while remaining approachable. Uniform stroke weights and open counters ensure legibility across all mediums.

| Weight | Use |
|--------|-----|
| Black | "Unlock" moments and bold statements. Emphasizes strength and reliability. |
| Bold | Headlines and section titles. |
| Regular | Standard text when using the primary typeface. |
| Italic | Details, captions, and subtle "behind-the-scenes" notes. Supports documentary-style imagery. |

### Secondary Typeface: Inter
A variable font family crafted for highly legible text on digital screens. Inter handles the heavy lifting of data, technical documentation, and long-form body copy. The go-to for utility — use whenever the primary goal is communication rather than "mood."

| Weight | Use |
|--------|-----|
| Extra Bold | High-emphasis digital headings. |
| Bold | Sub-headings and emphasis in body copy. |
| Regular | All primary paragraphs. Balanced proportions keep the optimistic feel even in dense text. |
| Medium / Semi-Bold | Labels, node descriptions, numerical data. Tabular figures ensure number alignment. |
| Light / Medium | Small text on graphics, subtle hover states in digital animations. |
| Italic | Callouts and differentiated inline text. |

### Logo Typeface: P22 Mackinac Pro
A versatile humanist serif that strikes a balance between historical charm and clean modern aesthetic. Used exclusively in the logo wordmark — do not use for body copy or headlines.

## Visual Style

### Graphic Identity
- Geometric illustrations and radiating data systems visualize the "unlock" metaphor
- Abstract-to-human bridge: pair geometric graphics with documentary photography

### Photography Style
- **Documentary realism** — bright, clean lighting, "builders in action"
- Prioritize real environments: hackathons, home offices, workshop spaces
- Highlight the optimistic, energetic, and slightly raw nature of human innovation
- Focus on the effort of the work

### Photography Don'ts
- No staged corporate photography
- No stock photography aesthetics
- No artificial diversity

### Design Mood
- Optimistic, authentic, and unmistakably human
- High-energy but not chaotic
- The cyan accent creates visual "sparks" — use it to signal moments of breakthrough or activation
