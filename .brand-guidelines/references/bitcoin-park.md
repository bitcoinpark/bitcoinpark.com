# Bitcoin Park — Complete Visual Identity Reference

Source: Bitcoin Park Brand Book 2025 (designed by Leah Dorrian / dorriandesigns)

---

## Brand Philosophy

Bitcoin Park is a community-supported campus in Nashville, TN and beyond, focused on grassroots bitcoin adoption and a home for Bitcoiners to work, learn, collaborate, and build.

The brand is modern, inviting, professional, and a creative take on traditional bitcoin themes. It represents connectedness, community, and the infinite possibilities of bitcoin.

---

## Logo System

### Primary Logo (Horizontal)
- Custom "b&p" graphic symbol + stylized "bitcoin park" letterwork
- The b&p letters intertwine to create a symbol of connectedness and community
- Inner shapes are an homage to the interior layout of Bitcoin Park
- The circular border signifies a coin — a metaphor for infinite possibilities
- The text has been carefully adjusted for a timeless, friendly, inviting, minimalistic, and elegant aesthetic
- Minimum clear space: 4px on all four sides
- Can be used in primary brand colors (dark green, orange, purple), plus dark grey and white

### Secondary Logo (Stacked)
- Same elements arranged vertically — brandmark above wordmark
- Use when horizontal layout is restricted by space or composition

### Brandmark (Icon Only)
- The circular b&p symbol alone
- For: social media profiles, watermarks, favicons, website footers

### Wordmark (Logotype Only)
- "bitcoin park" text only (all lowercase)
- For: when a typographic-only treatment is preferred

### Logo Don'ts
- Do not stretch
- Do not use random/unapproved colors
- Do not rotate
- Do not alter the text
- Do not change the brandmark
- Do not stylize (add effects, outlines, etc.)
- Do not change font
- Do not place on busy backgrounds
- Do not change proportions
- Do not add shadows

## Typography

### Primary Font: Optima
A humanist, modern sans-serif typeface that is refined yet tastefully stylized. Similar in style to the logo mark, creating a cohesive look. Primarily for headlines and brand personality.

| Weight | Use |
|--------|-----|
| Extra Bold | High-impact headlines. |
| Bold | Standard headlines and emphasis. |
| Regular | Supporting text when using the primary font. |
| Italic | Stylistic variation and callouts. |

**Note:** Optima may require a license for business use.

### Secondary Font: Source Sans Pro
A humanist sans-serif complementary to Optima. Open source and easily accessible. Best for body text, supporting headlines, or when Optima is unavailable.

| Weight | Use |
|--------|-----|
| Black | High-emphasis secondary headings. |
| Bold | Sub-headings and emphasis. |
| Regular | Body text and primary paragraphs. |
| Light | Captions, footnotes, and subtle text. |

## Imagery Style

### Illustration Approach
- Flat vector and line-work illustrations using brand palette
- Sophisticated, straightforward, minimalistic
- Color blocking with primary and secondary palettes
- Noise textures and subtle stylistic treatments are encouraged
- Maintain minimalism — let the concept be clear to the viewer

### Photography
- High-quality photos of real community, events, and spaces
- Hand-drawn illustration elements can complement photos
- Use brand colors as overlays or accents on photography

### Extension Principles
- **Standardized Visual Language:** Consistent application of primary palette, typography, logo usage, and graphic elements with subtle local adaptations
- **Unified Messaging and Tone:** All communication adheres to consistent tone and messaging strategy
- **Architectural Design Principles:** Recurring design motifs, material palettes, lighting, and spatial arrangements
- **Community Engagement Models:** Local leadership fosters organic growth while maintaining shared philosophy of education, collaboration, and innovation
