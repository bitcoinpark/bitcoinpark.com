Bitcoin payments are limited not by technology but by adoption density.

Parker Lewis (Zaprite) · Bitcoin Takeover 2026 · Presentation

The foundation of Bitcoin is fixed supply. People adopt Bitcoin as a store of value first. Buy a little for fiat, then more. But once you understand why Bitcoin stores value, accepting it as payment becomes logical.

That progression from savings to spending is already happening. The people who started saving in Bitcoin five or ten years ago are now advancing out to the frontier, accepting Bitcoin as payment. A small minority is using Bitcoin for transactions today, but the dollar is still the unit of account.

The future Zaprite is building toward is one where Bitcoin is the unit of account. Rib eyes priced in Bitcoin. Tickets to Bitcoin Park events priced in Bitcoin. Gas at the station priced in Bitcoin.

"It takes one person to buy Bitcoin, but if you want to pay someone in Bitcoin or they want to accept it, a trade takes two."

What limits Bitcoin payments is not tax policy, volatility, or fear that the price will go up forever. It is density. If only 1% of people understand Bitcoin, and you need two people to trade, the math is simple. As that 1% grows to 2%, 3%, 10%, the ability for Bitcoin to emerge as a trading economy grows exponentially with adoption.

But the adoption curve is steep. A doctor Parker Lewis respects said, "I like the idea of an independent currency, but until I can use Bitcoin to put gas in my car and food on the table, I'm out." That is a logical stance. Only a small minority can look at Bitcoin, read the Bitcoin Standard, and have the vision for why everyone in the future will demand it. The vast majority will come to understand Bitcoin when they see it used as money.

Commerce is key to adoption. But the market for Bitcoin payments in 2026 is brutal. 99% of people are uninterested in Bitcoin. That is the first blocker.

"Yes, we need builders all right! But not for fancy, novel, shining use cases. For trivial, boring, basic-bitch ones, which are still shitty!"

Giacomo Zucco posted that two days before Bitcoin Takeover. Parker Lewis added it to his deck last minute because it captures the exact problem Zaprite is solving.

Sending Bitcoin between friends still sucks. If you have 20 friends and each of them has six different wallets, you do not know which wallet to use when you want to send money. You do not know if your friend is willing to accept Lightning for $2,000. You do not know if their Lightning node has capacity. There is always this song and dance. Where do you want to be paid? Lightning or Bitcoin? Send me an address. Send me an invoice.

And what if wallets change? What if someone lost an app? How do you know?

"You do not have to have Zaprite to pay someone who's on Zaprite."

That is the key difference between Zaprite's P2P app and walled gardens like Venmo or Cash App. Zaprite is building non-custodially. Users can connect Strike, a Lightning node, a cold card, or any other wallet. The app facilitates discoverability, contact management, and payment routing without inserting Zaprite as a financial intermediary.

Network effects scale exponentially. If three wallets each had 100,000 users sending only to their own customers, putting all three together would create a 9x network effect compared to a single wallet. Every wallet will not become a P2P app for the same reason every bank is not a P2P app. Abstracting the identity layer and mapping wallets to a unified app lets you send to friends without knowing where they receive funds.

When you send money on Venmo, you have no idea what bank your friend uses. You do not care. You just need to know you are pointing at the right place.

"If we perfect the ways that peers pay other peers, then we can connect consumers directly to sellers and marketplaces."

The P2P app is strategic to broader payments. Once Zaprite perfects peer payments, they can enable one-click buys, auto-payments in Bitcoin, tap-to-pay, and online bill pay. It removes critical pain points for sellers at scale.

There is no good way to do refunds in Bitcoin. In the fiat world, you reverse a payment. In Bitcoin, whether Lightning or onchain, money has to be sent back. You need a destination to do that, and it has to tie to a sale in an automated way. Solving peer payments unlocks that.

The single thing limiting Bitcoin payments is that we need more Bitcoiners.

Zaprite focuses on Bitcoin businesses and their customers because that is where the greatest overlap exists between people willing to accept Bitcoin and a customer segment that wants to pay in it. When a business turns on Bitcoin payments, sales in Bitcoin might be 5%, growing to 10%. But if adoption is zero, that is a bad customer experience.

The closed beta for Zaprite's P2P app launches at the end of April. You can sign up at paymeandbitcoin.com. Open beta follows in the summer.

Density drives payments. Payments drive adoption. And the boring, basic infrastructure to make that loop work is still shitty. That is what Zaprite is building.
