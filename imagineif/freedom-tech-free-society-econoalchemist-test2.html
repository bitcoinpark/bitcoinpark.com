Freedom Tech is Fundamental to a Free Society

Econoalchemist · Imagine IF 2025 · Presentation

The Premise
"The laws of math are stronger than the laws of man. All the violence in the world doesn't change the laws of math."

Econoalchemist asked the room to raise their hands. Do you like to speak your mind? Do you like to transact freely? Do you own property? Do you value privacy? Every hand went up.

Then he flipped it. Under totalitarianism, every one of those rights disappears. Retaliation for speaking freely. Economic restrictions. Confiscation by force. Warrantless surveillance.

The bridge between those two worlds? Freedom tech. Software that's free and open source. Permissionless. Censorship resistant. Decentralized. "It's easy to contribute to. It's easy to find, but it's really hard to stop."

And the people who build it are the ones holding the line. "Without open-source developers, there is no freedom tech. And without freedom tech, there is no free society."

The Threats Are Already Here
"Combating misinformation is just code speak for attacking free speech, suppression of dissenting opinions, conditional access, and retaliation for speaking freely."

He walked through specific examples. The EU unveiled a digital identification mandate at the beginning of the month, wrapped in the language of combating misinformation. Google now requires government-issued ID from Play Store developers. They went further: if you want to load applications onto a stock Google device, you have to use the Play Store. No alternative sources.

Twitter is doing it too. In some countries, users have to upload government identification just to use the platform. And all of that KYC data creates honeypots that hackers and three-letter agencies can target, putting users in even more danger.

China's great firewall technology is being sold to other governments around the world. "When you hear internet regulations, that's just code speak for attacking journalists, activists, whistleblowers, dissidents."

Code Is Speech
"Bitcoin is not confiscatable if it's done right. And in its most simple form, Bitcoin is software. Software is code. Code is speech."

The Canadian trucker convoy was the case study. People stood up for bodily autonomy during COVID. The government responded by seizing bank accounts and going after donors. "This was all done under the guise of protecting democracy," he said. "That's code speak for divide and conquer society into smaller minority groups by discrediting and demonizing those that are dissenting."

Bitcoin sidesteps that. He laid out the stack: Ashigaru wallet on mobile, Sparrow on desktop, Ronin and Dojo for connecting to the network, collaborative transactions and CoinJoin for privacy on a public blockchain. "I will transact with whom I want when I want in whatever amount I want and I will not be restricted."

The countermeasures to surveillance run the same playbook. Nostr for identity you own. Start9 for self-hosted services. GrapheneOS and F-Droid for degoogled devices. Signal for encrypted communications. Tor, WireGuard, Mullvad for circumventing access controls. Your hardware, your rules.

The Pendulum Swings
"We don't go after gun manufacturers when some psychopath comes out of a crowd and shoots Charlie Kirk in the neck. Why are we going after software developers?"

The Samourai Wallet arrest was the sharpest example. Two privacy developers facing $200 million in fines and 25 years in prison for writing non-custodial wallet software. They never touched anyone's money. Never had custody. Never had control. The state kicked in their doors anyway.

He talked about the pendulum. One day it's perfectly legal to 3D print a gun in Colorado. The next day it's illegal. "One day you go from being an upstanding citizen to the next day you're labeled as a criminal. Once that label is put on you, the state is completely justified in coming down upon you with the full force."

Groups like petertodd.org and deterrence dispensed exist because the pendulum is always swinging. "Protecting privacy devs is how privacy tools keep getting built. And as Samourai Wallet themselves would say, privacy is a human fight."

Who's Going to Stop Us

He closed with a photo. Himself and his son on a roof, installing a Blockstream satellite dish to download the Bitcoin blockchain without the internet. His wife took the picture.

"I was explaining to my son that the question's not who's going to let us, but who's going to stop us."

Then the final slide. A list of open-source projects to support. "If you value the free society that you live in, then ensure that your kids can enjoy it too by supporting these developers today."

A father on a roof with his kid, pointed at a satellite, telling him the future is something you build. That's freedom tech in one image.
